How It Began

I have been engrossed in music since a young age. My parents started me playing the violin at the ripe age of three. I spent my adolesent years playing with a youth group called Minnesota Youth Symphonies (MYS), as well as with my school orchestra. During this time I had the privilage of playing yearly at Orchestra Hall. We would occasionally be supported by a local artist, or composer at these concerts. In my senior year with MYS, the Symphony Orchestra was even given the opportunity to play a tour in Cuba. I look back fondly on my time playing in orchestra, and believe those experiences made me the person I am today.

Trying New instruments

Once I reached my teen years I was interested in learning some other (more contemporary) instruments. I began with the guitar when I was 9.

Living The Dream

I am what you might call a creative. I also suffer from a condition called Yes Man Syndrome. This is what led to some of the most memorable times in my life so far.
